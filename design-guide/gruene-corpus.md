# Bündnis 90/Die Grünen — Visual Design Corpus

A reference corpus for design decisions in this workspace (flyers, zeitungen, posters). Every observation is judged against the only two criteria the user has set: **(1) human appeal** and **(2) information transport**. Where both strong and weak examples exist, both are documented — contrast is more useful than catalog.

This is a *reference* document, not a rulebook. If the corpus and the CD-Quickguide disagree on a layout question, prefer what the corpus shows working in the wild. The CD-Quickguide encodes brand compliance; the corpus encodes what humans respond to.

---

## 0. Brand Baseline (from `260313_DieGrünen_CD-Quickguide.pdf`)

These are the brand-compliance constraints, not aesthetic guidance. Every corpus entry below assumes these as the floor.

**Colors (only these four — secondary palette is a deliberate restriction):**
- Dunkelgrün `#257639` · RGB 37/118/57 · CMYK 85/35/95/10 · Pantone 2465 C
- Hellgrün `#56af31` · RGB 86/175/49 · CMYK 69/0/100/0 · Pantone 3501 C
- Gelb `#ffed00` · RGB 255/240/0 · CMYK 0/0/100/0 · Pantone 102 C
- Magenta `#e6007e` · RGB 225/0/120 · CMYK 0/100/0/0 · Pantone 225 C

**Typography:**
- **Gotham Narrow Ultra** — headlines (sans, very bold)
- **Gotham Narrow Book** — sublines, body (sans, regular)
- **Vollkorn Black Italic** — emphasis inside headlines, pull-quotes (serif, italic, heavy)

**Type rules:**
- Zeilenabstand (line height) = font size × 0.9 for headlines
- HL/SL gap = `X × 2` where X is x-height
- HL/Copy gap = `X × 2`
- Fließtext line-height = font size × 1.3

**Layout rules:**
- M (margin) = `0.06 × short edge of trim`
- Logo size = `3 × M` (print) or `2.5 × M` (digital)
- Schutzzone of M around logo — no element inside it
- **Typography always in combination with green.** Type sits on a green Farbfläche (Dunkelgrün or Hellgrün), never floating on white. Type-on-photo is allowed only if the person/subject is unambiguously *in front of* a green plate or *photographed against* a green ground.

**Gestaltungselemente (accents):**
- Yellow circles drawn around words for emphasis (hand-drawn feel)
- Yellow underlines under words
- Magenta "Störer" — pink circles or pink rectangles with a yellow Wahlkreuz, used for date/event call-outs

This is the floor. Everything below builds on top.

## 1. Posters (Wahlplakate)

### 1.1 The 2025 "Ein Mensch. Ein Wort." poster system (Jung von Matt)

**Source:** [gruene.de — Unsere Motive zur Bundestagswahl](https://www.gruene.de/artikel/unsere-motive-zur-bundestagswahl-2025) · [absatzwirtschaft analysis](https://www.absatzwirtschaft.de/so-inszeniert-jung-von-matt-die-neue-wahlkampagne-der-gruenen-271074/) · [politik-kommunikation critique](https://www.politik-kommunikation.de/politik/kampagnen-watch-teil-3-buendnis90-die-gruenen/)

**Mechanic:** Each poster owns one word, gigantic, set against a person. Habeck → "Zuversicht." Baerbock → "Zusammen." Themenplakate own verb-ending action words ("Bezahlbar machen!", "Schützen!", "Sanieren!", "Sichern!"). The tagline "Ein Mensch. Ein Wort." holds the system together — present in every motiv, but small.

**Layout pattern:**
- Full-bleed Dunkelgrün or near-monochrome background
- Portrait sized large but not full-bleed — typically upper two-thirds, framed with breathing room
- Headline word in Gotham Narrow Ultra at heroic size (≥1/3 of poster height) below or beside the portrait
- Tagline "Ein Mensch. Ein Wort." set small in Gotham Narrow Book
- Logo at bottom-right, sized to the brand-rule `3M`
- Yellow accent circle/underline used sparingly on the headline word (carrying the sketch-energy of the CD-Quickguide example)

**Verdict — human appeal: mixed.** Praised for being "progressive and bold" with "clear visual reduction." Criticized as "bloodless," "joyless," "intellectually overthinking" — the monochrome reduction reads as cool intelligence, not warmth. The campaign drove record membership growth (30 000+ new members) but failed to mobilize beyond the already-convinced. **Lesson for this workspace:** monochrome reduction is high-status but cold; if you reduce, compensate with portrait expression, environmental warmth, or a yellow accent that breaks the monochrome.

**Verdict — information transport: strong.** The single-word mechanic is one of the cleanest information designs in recent German political advertising. You read the poster from 30 m away in under a second: word + person. Tagline + party identity load second. Critics object to vagueness of *meaning* ("Zuversicht in what?"), not to the design's clarity — the *transport* works; the *content* leaves room for ambiguity. **Lesson:** the one-thing-per-surface discipline is the single most important transport rule. A panel that tries to say three things says nothing.

### 1.2 The 2021 "Bereit, weil ihr es seid" poster system

**Source:** [gruene.de Kampagne 2021](https://www.gruene.de/artikel/unsere-kampagne-bereit-weil-ihr-es-seid) · [Horizont gallery 2021](https://www.horizont.net/galerien/Wahlplakate-der-Gruenen-4379) · [taz critique](https://taz.de/Wahlkampagne-der-Gruenen/!5781143/)

**Mechanic:** Slogan-driven, not single-word. Posters carry full sentences ("Wohlstand ist, wenn alle Kinder ohne Armut aufwachsen.") in headline-italic. Personalities (Baerbock + Habeck, then later individual citizens) appear in candid, smiling, environmental portraits — not studio.

**Layout pattern:**
- Portrait dominates the upper half, often with environmental context (sunflower fields, urban streets)
- Hellgrün or Dunkelgrün plate occupies the lower third for the slogan
- Slogan typeset partly in Gotham Narrow Ultra and partly in Vollkorn Black Italic — the italic stress carries emotion, the bold carries substance
- Yellow underline or yellow circle drawn around the emotionally-loaded word
- Tagline "Bereit, weil ihr es seid." consistent across all motifs

**Verdict — human appeal: strong but inconsistent.** When the portraits are environmental and candid, the posters read warm and inviting — closer to "person you'd meet at a market" than "candidate." Where studio portraits were used, the warmth collapses. **Lesson:** environmental portraits beat studio portraits for this party's brand. The hand of the photographer matters more than the cleanness of the headshot.

**Verdict — information transport: medium.** Sentences are longer and more complex than 2025's words; the design works at 5 m, not 30 m. The italic+bold split inside one headline gives the eye a focal word but slows reading. **Lesson:** if a slogan is longer than one word, *promote* one word inside it via italic or color — never set all words equal.

### 1.3 Local candidate posters (KV/OV scale)

**Source:** [gruene-birkenfeld.de Plakate](https://gruene-birkenfeld.de/wahlen/bundestagswahl-2025/unsere-plakate) · [gruene-unterfranken.de](https://gruene-unterfranken.de/die-plakate-sind-da/) · candidate sites linked from these.

**Pattern (the strong examples):** Candidate portrait fills the left half or upper two-thirds full-bleed (no white frame). Dunkelgrün or Hellgrün plate fills the remainder for name + claim + logo. Name set in Vollkorn Black Italic large, claim set in Gotham Narrow Ultra below, logo bottom-right.

**Pattern (the weak examples):** Portrait floats inside white margin. Name set on white below in Dunkelgrün — clean but feels like a CV photo, not a campaign poster. Slogans abstract ("Für unsere Zukunft") rather than specific ("Klimaplan jetzt für Mödling").

**Verdict — human appeal: portrait commitment is binary.** Either the portrait is full-bleed with confident green plates around it, or it reads as institutional. There is no middle ground. The current workspace falzflyer cover sits in the institutional zone — see §6.

**Verdict — information transport: claim-specificity matters more than layout.** "Bezahlbares Wohnen für Bremerhaven" transports more than "Für eine bessere Zukunft," regardless of typography. The design's job is to *not get in the way* of a sharp claim. **Lesson:** if the claim is generic, no design can rescue it. Push back on generic claims at the content stage.

## 2. Flyers, Postkarten, Falzflyer

### 2.1 The "Themen-Faltblatt" pattern

**Source pattern observed across:** `gruene-niedersachsen.de`, `gruene-nrw.de` downloadable PDFs (linked from [gruene-niedersachsen.de Bundestagswahl 2025](https://gruene-niedersachsen.de/bundestagswahl-2025/)), Bremen and Hessen state PDFs.

**Strong pattern:**
- Cover panel: full-bleed photo (often environmental — bicycle on Innenstadt-Straße, family in park) with Dunkelgrün plate occupying bottom 40% for headline. Headline 1–2 lines max. No body copy on cover.
- Inner spread: each Thema gets its own panel — never two themes per panel. Panel = one icon + one headline + one short body of 30–50 words.
- Closer panel: vollflächig Dunkelgrün, Wahlkreuz visual or QR code, date, URL.

**Weak pattern (seen in older or amateur Faltblätter):** all themes crammed onto one inner panel with bullet list. The list reads as a checklist with no visual rhythm; nothing is privileged.

**Verdict — human appeal:** strong panel-per-theme designs feel generous (white space + photo) and read as care for the reader. List-of-bullets designs feel rushed and read as content dumped. **Lesson:** the user's critique of the workspace falzflyer middle list ("just put there without thought") is the canonical failure mode of this category. Symptoms: items centered with even spacing, no item visually privileged, no anchor connecting the list to the panel headline.

**Verdict — information transport:** one-thing-per-panel transports better even when total information is identical. Five themes across five panels, with one micro-photo each, is read; five themes on one panel is skipped. **Lesson:** if the format gives you N panels, use N panels. Do not save space by stacking.

### 2.2 Information density discipline

Across all professional Grüne flyer materials, **per-panel word counts are low**. Themen panels typically run:
- Headline: 4–8 words
- Subline / claim: 6–12 words
- Body: 30–60 words, rarely over 80
- One photo, one icon, or one Stör-element — never all three

When designs add a fourth element, density rises and human appeal drops. The 2021 campaign deck (page-set linked from the PDF program) holds this discipline; the workspace's current Themen panels (per `improvements/05-kandidat-falzflyer.md`) overload some panels and underload others — the inconsistency is the visible failure, not the absolute density.

**Lesson:** treat any panel with more than three distinct content elements as suspect. Cut, don't shrink.

### 2.3 Headline tone

Across the corpus, three distinct headline tones appear:

- **Assertive imperative** ("Schützen!", "Bezahlbar machen!", "Klimaplan jetzt.") — used on 2025 themen-plakate. Reads urgent and competent. Best for closer/CTA panels and for issue posters.
- **Invitational declarative** ("Wohlstand ist, wenn …", "Bereit, weil ihr es seid.") — used on 2021 sentence-headlines. Reads warm and inclusive. Best for cover panels and for tone-setting.
- **Questioning** ("Was ich für Mödling will") — rare in professional Grüne work. When used (mostly on candidate Webseiten not on print), it sits next to a candidate's quote. Risky in print: it implies the answer is below, then the answer must deliver.

**Lesson:** match tone to panel function. A cover that says "Was ich für Mödling will" sets up an expectation the inner panels must pay off; if the inner panels are a generic bullet list, the cover question is a broken contract. The workspace falzflyer cover currently does this (see §6).

## 3. Zeitungen & Magazines (Heinrich-Böll-Stiftung & similar)

**Source:** [boell.de Publikationen](https://www.boell.de/de/publikationen) · [Böll.Thema magazine](https://www.boell.de/de/boellthema) · CD background by [Blotto Design](https://blottodesign.de/navigation/index-blotto-263.html) (1998 CD; modernized 2008).

The Böll-Stiftung is not Die Grünen, but its visual language is the closest sibling — same audience, same intellectual register, often same designers in the orbit. It is the right reference for `zeitung-a4-grun`.

**Pattern:**
- Generous whitespace; magazine-grade information density
- Two- or three-column grid; columns rarely full-justified, almost always left-aligned ragged
- Headlines mix Gotham (or its closest cousin in their CD) with a serif italic for emphasis — same Vollkorn-style move
- Strong photographic openings on chapter spreads (full-bleed double-page); body text panels strictly grid-aligned afterwards
- Color blocks used as section dividers, not as decoration; one section = one accent color
- Pull-quotes set in serif italic, magenta or yellow accent

**Verdict — human appeal:** the whitespace discipline is the dominant factor. A page that breathes feels intelligent and trustworthy; a page that crowds feels desperate. This holds across every issue.

**Verdict — information transport:** consistent grid means the eye learns the layout in 2–3 spreads and reads faster after that. Inconsistent grids (where columns shift width per spread without reason) cost reading speed. **Lesson for the workspace zeitung:** one decision per spread (full-bleed photo, two-column body, single-pull-quote spread, etc.) — not three.

## 4. Web & Social

**Source:** [gruene.de](https://www.gruene.de/) · [gruene-niedersachsen.de](https://gruene-niedersachsen.de/) · party Instagram (@die_gruenen), MP and MEP accounts.

### 4.1 Web

- Modular block layout: hero with one image + one headline + one CTA, then a stack of 3-up content rows
- Typography larger than typical German news sites — confidence move, also accessibility
- Buttons: rounded rectangle, Dunkelgrün fill, white text, ample padding
- Photography: real people in real environments (farmers, patients, families) — never stock photography. The "real environment" rule is the strongest single signal of brand-aligned imagery.

### 4.2 Social (Instagram)

- 1:1 and 4:5 frames most common
- Type-only "quote cards" use Dunkelgrün or Hellgrün full-bleed with Vollkorn Italic centered headline + Gotham Book attribution. These are the simplest assets in the system and consistently the highest-engagement.
- Photo cards crop tight on a single face; expression is candid (mid-speech, half-smile), never stiff portrait
- Yellow underline or yellow circle accents survive into social — a key continuity signal

**Lesson:** the social system is essentially a *compressed* version of the print system. If a print flyer cover would not work as a 1:1 Instagram crop, it's probably under-committed visually.

## 5. Photography style

Across the corpus, four conventions appear consistently and one breaks them.

**Convention 1: environmental, not studio.** Candidate portraits taken in the candidate's actual environment (Markt, Werkstatt, Bibliothek, vor dem Rathaus) are the standard. Backdrop is rarely sharp — depth of field puts the person forward.

**Convention 2: mid-action expression.** Mid-sentence, half-smile, looking slightly off-camera. Never the symmetrical CV-photo smile. A laugh-line or thinking-look reads as competence + warmth; a posed smile reads as candidate.

**Convention 3: lighting is daylight or warm interior — never flash.** Top-light or window-light on the face. Cool fluorescent light is absent.

**Convention 4: framing is medium-tight.** Head + shoulders + a hint of environmental context. A floating-head crop reads as ID-card.

**Anti-convention (the failure mode):** floating-head studio crop on white. Reads as institutional, not human. The workspace falzflyer P1 currently sits here — Convention 4 violated.

**Verdict — human appeal:** environmental beats studio every time, even at lower technical quality. A grainy candid in real light beats a sharp posed studio crop. **Lesson:** if asked to choose between portrait quality and portrait realism, choose realism.

**Verdict — information transport:** photo style sets the *tone* the headline is read in. A studio-crop next to "Mut zur Klima-Wende" reads as performative; the same headline next to a candid market-stall portrait reads as committed. The same words land differently based on the picture.

## 6. The current workspace, against this corpus

Direct read of `templates/kandidat-falzflyer-din-lang/page-01.png` (front side, panels P1/P2/P3) using the corpus as a yardstick. This is the design the user critiqued in conversation.

**P1 (Cover) — what's working and what's not:**
- ✅ Layout *structure* is correct: portrait + name + slogan stacked vertically
- ❌ Logo top-left at ~20 mm is below the 38 mm Print-Soll (CD §6) and reads as visually weak — the eye doesn't register party identity until the headline does the work
- ❌ Portrait is a tight headshot crop on what reads as a neutral interior — Convention 4 (medium-tight + environment) violated. Reads as ID-card not candidate
- ❌ Name "Maria Beispiel" set in Vollkorn Italic on white plate below portrait — §7 of CD says type lives on green; this is the brand-rule border-case, and the corpus universally lands on the green-plate side
- ❌ Slogan "Mut zur Klima-Wende. Für Mödling." set small below the name — competing with the name for attention, neither reads as primary
- **Single highest-leverage fix:** commit the cover to a green-plate footer holding name + slogan, and use a portrait with an inch of environmental context (not a tight headshot)

**P2 (Mein Plan) — the panel the user specifically called out:**
- ✅ Hellgrün full-bleed background — §7 satisfied
- ✅ White headline at top — readable, well-anchored
- ❌ The five-item list ("Klimaplan jetzt." / "Leistbares Wohnen." / etc.) is centered, evenly-spaced, all items typographically identical. **This is the canonical failure mode from §2.1.** Eye has nowhere to land first; no item is privileged; the list reads as filler not voice
- ❌ Items end in periods, mimicking sentence-headlines, but each is only 2–3 words — the period implies finality the content can't pay off. Either commit to short verb-phrases without periods, or commit to full sentences
- ❌ Headline "Was ich für Mödling will" is a question (§2.3 questioning tone), but the answer below is a list — the answer-format doesn't satisfy the question-format. A reader reads "what I want" and expects a paragraph, gets bullets
- **Single highest-leverage fix:** privilege one item visually (larger size, yellow accent, or the only one in italic) and demote the others. Or cut to three items with body text below each. Even spacing across five peers is the bug.

**P3 (Wahltag) — the panel that mostly works:**
- ✅ Vollflächig Dunkelgrün — §7 satisfied with the strongest possible commitment
- ✅ Yellow Wahlkreuz upper third → gives the eye an anchor on entry
- ✅ "Wähle Grün am 23. Mai" in Gotham Bold White — clear hierarchy, the right size
- ✅ Date in Vollkorn Italic Yellow — uses the emphasis style for what should be emphasized
- ⚠️ URL `gruene-moedling.at` floats at the bottom with too much gap to the date — the panel breaks rhythm at the bottom
- **This panel is the closest to corpus-level work in the current output, and it's the one that committed hardest to the brand rules (full-bleed Dunkelgrün, yellow Wahlkreuz, proper hierarchy).** The lesson: half-commitment underperforms full-commitment, even at the same effort cost.

**Pattern across all three panels:** the design is competent on technique (alignments are clean, brand colors are right, fonts are correct) but undercommitted on *intent*. P1 is half ID-card, half campaign poster. P2 is half list, half voice. Only P3 commits fully — and only P3 reads as professional. **Lesson for future work in this repo: pick a posture per panel and commit to it. The undercommitted panel is always weaker than either of the two committed alternatives, regardless of which alternative.**

## 7. Recurring strengths

What consistently lands in the Grüne corpus:

1. **One-thing-per-surface.** The 2025 single-word mechanic and the panel-per-theme flyer pattern share this DNA. A poster that says one thing well outperforms a poster that says three things competently.
2. **Type lives on green.** §7 of the CD is not just a brand rule — it is the visual signature of the party. Every successful design in the corpus commits to it. Every weak one is the border-case (type on white).
3. **Yellow as accent, not as area.** Yellow appears as circles, underlines, single words — never as background plates. This restraint is what gives yellow its punch when it is used.
4. **Environmental candid portraits.** Real people in real environments. Convention 4 is the single highest-correlation visual signal of brand-aligned design in the corpus.
5. **Italic-Vollkorn for emphasis, not for body.** The serif italic is reserved for the emotionally-loaded word or the pull-quote — never used at length. This restraint is what makes it carry weight when used.
6. **Tagline holds the system.** "Ein Mensch. Ein Wort." / "Bereit, weil ihr es seid." — the tagline appears small on every motif and unifies the system. Workspace materials currently lack a unifying tagline across templates; consider adding one.
7. **Magenta Stör-elements for dates and events only.** Pink circles or pink rectangles with a yellow Wahlkreuz are reserved for the action moment (date, deadline, sign-up). Using magenta for non-action accents weakens the system.
8. **Generous whitespace at magazine scale.** The Böll-Stiftung publications and the gruene.de homepage share this — whitespace = competence + trust. Not an aesthetic preference; a credibility signal.

## 8. Recurring weaknesses (failure modes to avoid)

What consistently fails:

1. **Type on white.** The §7 border-case always reads as institutional, never as campaign. Even when alignment is perfect.
2. **Floating-head studio portraits.** ID-card crop on neutral background. Reads as CV photo, not candidate.
3. **Even-spaced peer lists.** Multiple items typographically identical with even vertical spacing — no item privileged, no rhythm, no anchor. The user's exact critique of the workspace falzflyer P2.
4. **Undersized logos.** When logo is smaller than the brand-rule `3M`, party identity registers late. Reads as accidentally-Grüne, not deliberately-Grüne.
5. **Generic claims.** "Für eine bessere Zukunft" cannot be rescued by typography. Push back at content stage.
6. **Tone-mismatch between question headline and bullet answer.** Question implies paragraph; bullets imply checklist. Mismatched tone breaks the reading contract.
7. **Half-commitment.** A panel that's almost-full-bleed-Dunkelgrün, or a portrait that's almost-environmental, or a slogan that's almost-specific — all underperform either alternative. The corpus rewards conviction.
8. **Cool monochrome without warmth compensation.** The 2025 campaign's main critique. If you reduce, compensate — yellow accent, environmental portrait, or warm headline tone. Reduction without warmth reads as cold.

## 9. Open corpus gaps

Things this corpus cannot yet answer, that need user-rated examples to close:

- **Per-Land aesthetic differences.** Bavaria-Grüne, NRW-Grüne and Berlin-Grüne posters look subtly different from each other. The corpus does not yet rank these.
- **Photography commissioning brief.** The "environmental candid" rule is observed but not specified — what lens, what light, what direction-of-gaze. A user-supplied "yes/no" rated set of 10 candidate portraits would close this.
- **Information density per format.** The user's tolerance for body copy density at A4-Zeitung scale vs DIN-lang Falzflyer scale is unknown. Five user-rated existing zeitung pages would calibrate this.
- **Tagline adoption.** Whether to commission a workspace-wide tagline (the role "Ein Mensch. Ein Wort." plays at federal scale) or leave each template ungrouped — open user decision.

These are the highest-value additions a user-rated corpus expansion could make.
