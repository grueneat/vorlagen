Strategy summary

Three layout regressions from the c8bg0 Barlow + Vollkorn font swap share one root mechanism: stacked single-line headline frames use Scribus FLOP=1, so the visible gap between two lines is Δy − ascent(font_N) + ascent(font_N+1). The old y_mm were frozen for Gotham's ascent; Barlow/Vollkorn ascents differ, so the top gap collapses (the "dreizeilige" case).

  • Fix the mechanism once, not 12 templates by hand.

    A shared emitter helper drives each frame's y_mm from the real fc-matched TTF ascent (fontTools), so gaps are even by construction across all 12 templates.

  • Same fix class for zeitung-a4 and the icons.

    zeitung leading → safe 30–32pt window; falzflyer inline icons → scale_type=0 / re-pack at source.

  • Verify post-c8bg0 state FIRST.

    This worktree's HEAD may predate the c8bg0 merge; measuring on stale Gotham metrics would mis-tune everything.

  • Baselines only after human visual sign-off.

    A focused headline-spacing review gates promotion, mirroring c8bg0 — Task 6 is a human-verify checkpoint.

03Decisions

Key decisions & options

How to correct the stacked-headline gaps

Chosen

A metric-driven corrector in the shared emitter, solving frame_top_{k+1} = frame_top_k + linesp − ascent(font_{k+1}) + ascent(font_k) from real fc-matched ascents — even gaps by construction, generalizes to all 12 templates.

Hand-edit each y_mm per template — 12+ fragile constants that re-break on the next font change (exactly what broke here).

Collapse to one multi-line frame — re-introduces the Scribus mixed-font per-line-leading mis-placement the split exists to avoid. Out of scope.

Falzflyer social-icon root cause

Disambiguate at render

Two source-fixable candidates: (a) Scribus 1.6.x white-on-transparent RGBA invisible under SCALETYPE=1; (b) a broken inline blob. Render one falzflyer, inspect SLA-vs-PNG, fix with scale_type=0 and/or re-pack via pack_inline_image.

When to promote baselines

Gated on human review

Promotion happens only after a focused, documented visual review of headline spacing across all affected templates (Task 6) — never in the execute tasks.
